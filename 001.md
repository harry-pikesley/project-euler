# Problem 1

## Problem Description

If we list all the natural numbers below 10 that are multiples of 3 or 5, we get 3, 5, 6 am 9. The sum of these multiples is 23.

Find the sum of all the multiples of 3 or 5 below 1000.

## Problem Solution

We wish to sum all multiples of 3 or 5 below or 1000, but to avoid duplicates, we must also subtract the sum of all common multiples of 3 and 5 below 1000, that is, the multiples of 15 below 1000.

Note that there are 333 multiples of 3, 199 multiples of 5 and 66 multiples of 15 below 1000. So, we evaluate

$$
\begin{aligned}
\sum_{n=1}^{333} 3n + \sum_{n=1}^{199} 5n - \sum_{n=1}^{66} 15n &= 3 \sum_{n=1}^{333} n + 5 \sum_{n=1}^{199} n - 15 \sum_{n=1}^{66} n \\
 &= 3 \times \frac{333 \times 334}{2} + 5 \times \frac{199 \times 200}{2} - 15 \times \frac{66 \times 67}{2} \\
 &= 233168
\end{aligned}
$$

## Final Answer

233168 (Incorrect)
